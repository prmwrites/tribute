Publius Clodius Pulcher

"It is enough, I hope, to say that, for all his complications and for all his failures, Clodius mattered- briefly, but intensely.
-W. Jeffrey Tatum, 'The Patrician Tribune'
  • 93 BC- Clodius is born to a prominent Roman family, the Claudii.
  • 68 BC- Clodius is posted as a military staffer to a general (his brother-in-law) of an Eastern Roman legion.
  • 67 BC-Clodius nearly, but unsuccessfully, mutinies his in-laws army.
  • 66 BC- A different brother in law gives Clodius command of a small fleet to hunt pirates. Clodius is captured by the pirates, who ransom him to the King of Cyprus. The King sends such an embarressingly small sum for Clodius' release, the pirates laugh and free him anyways.
  • 65 BC- Returns to Rome, was handed the prosecution of a Roman governor Cataline, who was being charged with extortion and other influence peddling crimes. Colludes to acquit him of all charges.
  • 63 BC- Sides against the conspirators in the a coup d'etat, is part of the young retinue that acts as Cicero's bodyguard during the proceedings.
  • 62 BC- Clodius, dressed as a woman, intrudes upon the rites of the Good Goddess, which were being held at the house of Julius Caesar. Clodius was allegedly there to seduce the wife of Caesar.
  • 62 BC- Lucullus brings up Clodius on charges on incestum, for allegedly sleeping with Lucullus' wife (Clodius' sister). Caesar spoke in Clodius' favor, and Crassus, one of Rome's wealthiest men, bribed the jury en masse to ensure Clodius' acquital.
